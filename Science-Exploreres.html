What is Science?

Science is a systematic way of studying the natural world through observation, experimentation, and reasoning. It is the pursuit of knowledge about how things work, helping us explain events in nature and use that understanding to make predictions.

Branches of Science

Science is broadly categorized into three foundational branches, each with its own focus and methodologies:

  1. Formal Sciences
    2.

    These explore abstract systems through logic and mathematical structures. They include fields such as:

    • Mathematics
    • Logic
    • Statistics
    • Computer Science

  2. Natural Sciences
    3.

    Focused on the physical and biological world, this branch divides further into:

    • Physical Sciences(inanimate matter and energy):

      • Physics
      • Chemistry
      • Astronomy
      • Earth Science

    • Life Sciences - Exploring living organisms, including:
      • Biology
      • Zoology
      • Botany
      • Microbiology

Interdisciplinary Sciences

These fields combine elements from multiple branches of science to address complex problems:

  • Environmental Science
  • Biochemistry
  • Neuroscience
  • Astrobiology
